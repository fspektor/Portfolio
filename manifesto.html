    • "A Cyborg Design Manifesto" is original research thesis, completed through UC Berkeley’s Interdisciplinary Studies program.

    I conducted design ethnographies with a unique cross-section of Bay Area populations: leaders from the disability community, the biohacking movement, and the designers & engineers of Silicon Valley. My research sought to understand how market forces shape socially valuable bodies, specifically under the priorities of emerging biotechnologies.

    Using content analysis on technologies from nutritional supplements to cyborg implants, I synthesized a manifesto on ethical practices for design. With interview insight, then I co-created speculative artifacts which suggest alternative visions of assistive / bio-technologies.

    My research interests in this space include the ethics of embodiment, technology, labor, and community-driven design methodologies.
