    Design Researcher

    A perpetually curious maker, exploring the messy margins and the hard-to-talk-about spaces.

    My work seeks to change the discourse around disability, sexuality, and technology through community-driven design methods. I care deeply about the ethics of embodiment; I center my research on the ways that technological systems and market forces construct socially valuable bodies. I believe design is the manifestation of our ideals.

    My list of favorite things includes starting new projects in the makerspace, blues dancing, and collecting my grandfather's Soviet anecdotes.

    Bioethics & Design graduate from UC Berkeley, currently building sex education models for the blind & visually impaired.
